AI Automation Micro-Agency
— foundation notes

The thinking, the math, and the 30-day plan behind FlowIQ — a small, 4-person AI automation studio serving Bay Area small businesses.

Project · FlowIQ Under · Magic Masala Group Status · Early build
AI Automation SMB Services Bay Area 4-Person Team

Why this, why now

Over 70% of U.S. small businesses still handle invoicing, lead follow-up, scheduling, and customer service by hand. They know AI exists. They've heard about ChatGPT. They don't know where to start, and they don't want to learn a new tool. They just want the repetitive work gone.

A four-person team can comfortably serve 8–12 clients on monthly retainer with a few hours of work per client per week. The work is reusable across clients (the same Zapier + Claude scaffolding gets cloned), and the recurring-revenue model means month one is the only hard month.

"You do nothing. We replace the boring parts of your week with quiet AI that just works." — the FlowIQ pitch in one sentence.

Setup basics

  • Startup cost: $500–$2,000 (Zapier/Make subscription ~$50/mo, Claude/ChatGPT API ~$20–100/mo, website ~$200–500, LLC ~$100–800)
  • Weekly effort: 5–10 hours per person initially
  • Net margin: 70–85% — most cost is API + tooling, not headcount
  • Pricing: $1,500 / $2,000 / $2,500 monthly tiers

How the four people split it

  • Person 1 — CEO / Sales (Ribesh): Find clients, close deals, own relationships, point of contact for retainer accounts
  • Person 2 — Operations: Build and maintain automation workflows, monitor for breakage
  • Person 3 — Content / Marketing: LinkedIn content, outreach, case studies, inbound funnel
  • Person 4 — Finance / Client Success: Invoicing, weekly check-ins, upsells, renewals

The main risk

The honest risk is churn. Clients cancel when automations break and nobody fixes them fast. The fix is built into the offer: every workflow has a weekly health check, and the retainer covers "one fix per month guaranteed." That single line turns the agency from "set it and pray" into "we own it."

30-day action plan

Week 1 — Foundation

  • Register LLC ($100–800)
  • Grab a domain (current candidate: flowiq.co or similar)
  • Set up LinkedIn for the agency arm
  • Stand up the live site at ribeshbk.github.io/flowiq

Week 2 — Outreach

  • Identify 20 local Bay Area small businesses
  • DM each with the same offer: a free 30-minute "AI audit"
  • Aim for 3–5 audit calls

Week 3 — Convert

  • Close 1–2 pilots at the Starter tier ($500–1,500/mo)
  • Build the first workflow live, in a shared doc, so the client watches it happen

Week 4 — Multiply

  • Screenshot real results from the pilot
  • Write a 1-page case study
  • Use it as the hook for the next 50 outreach DMs at the Growth tier
